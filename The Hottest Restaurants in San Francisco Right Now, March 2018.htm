Where to eat right now

by Ellen Fort@ellenfork Updated Mar 1, 2018, 12:43pm PST

Tipsters, readers, friends and family of Eater usually all have the same question: Where should I eat right now? Restaurant obsessives want to know what's new, what's hot, which favorite chef just launched a sophomore effort, where to sip the cocktail of the moment. And while the Eater 38 is a crucial resource covering old standbys and neighborhood essentials across the city, it is not a chronicle of the 'it' places of the moment. Thus, we offer the Eater Heatmap, which will change continually to highlight where the foodie crowds are flocking at the moment — even if that might be an older spot.

January’s newcomers were Kaya, Ippudo, and dosa by Dosa. Now welcome Nyum Bai and Funky Elephant.

Check out the map of the Bay Area's hottest restaurants right now below (ordered North to South, not ranked), and drop any feedback in the comments section.

For all the latest San Francisco dining intel, subscribe to Eater SF's newsletter.

1 Funky Elephant

From the former chef de cuisine of SF’s Hawker Fare, Funky Elephant is bringing very authentic versions of owners Nanchaphon Laptanachai and Supasit Puttikaew’s native Thai cuisine to Berkeley. Isaan-style pork sausage, khao mun ghai, and Bangkok-style pad Thai studded with prawns are a few of the items on the menu. Make sure to order the green curry beef, which is made with perfectly cooked hangar steak. The small space, formerly Doughnut Dolly, has limited seating, but the entire menu is available for takeout and delivery as well.

1313 Ninth Street Suite 120
Berkeley, CA 94710

2 Cdp

Piedmont’s two-Michelin-starred Commis now has a younger sibling, with a cozy bar adjacent to its hushed dining room. Cdp (for chef de partie) has a strong menu of cocktails, Champagnes, and brandies, as well as upscale but simple bar bites. That includes beef tartare topped with beef fat lavash, and a sandwich described by the chef as a “greasy bar sandwich” with pressed taleggio cheese, speck, toasted caraway butter, and raw orange blossom honey.

Oakland, CA 94611

3 dosa By DOSA

It’s a bright and fast-paced addition to Oakland’s Uptown neighborhood, with a menu of South Indian favorites from its bigger siblings across the Bay. The quick-service version of Anjan and Emily Mitra’s popular Dosas also has a full bar with creative cocktails incorporating Indian flavors that make it an excellent happy hour or dinner stop. Or, go in the a.m. for breakfast dosas filled with eggs.

2301 Broadway
Oakland, CA 94612

4 Eight Tables by George Chen

Above Chinatown’s bustling China Live restaurant and market, there’s a serene escape in the form of Eight Tables, a fine-dining restaurant based on the private chateau cuisine of China (called si fang cai). Restaurateur and chef George Chen based the restaurant on dining experiences in homes of the elite in China, and he spared no expense in its design. The multi-course menu ($225) includes luxurious versions of Chinese dishes like foie gras dumplings, and har gow with caviar. If excellent dumplings and a much lower bill are more your thing, stay downstairs for lunch or dinner at China Live.

8 Kenneth Rexroth Pl
San Francisco, CA 94133

5 The Snug

A talented group of young industry vets has opened what’s definitely Pac Height’s hippest new bar. It’s got great craft cocktails, a local beer list, small producer wines by the glass, and a menu of upscale bar food from a former sous chef at Benu. It’s definitely bringing some much-needed buzz to a rather sedate neighborhood.

2301 Fillmore St
San Francisco, CA 94115

SHN Orpheum Theatre

The Orpheum Theater was built for theater circuit owner Alexander Pantages and opened its doors in 1926. The building has high, vaulted ceilings and a grand atmosphere, making it the perfect venue for a live performance like Finding Neverland or Hamilton. 6 International Smoke

As the old saying goes, “Everything Ayesha Curry touches is fire.” That includes International Smoke, the new collaboration between Curry and prolific chef Michael Mina. The SoMa barbecue restaurant has ribs for days, including American barbecue, Cuban “mojo” and Korean gochujang. The extensive menu also includes a magnificent seafood platter, wagyu shaking beef, and more deluxe dishes with fun tableside flourishes — for more details, and some critiques, see Eater SF critic Rachel Levin’s review — Cocktails and wine list are great too. Reservations are limited but there’s space for walk-ins, so don’t get discouraged.

301 Mission St
San Francisco, CA 94105
(415) 543-7474

7 IPPUDO San Francisco

At long last, San Francisco has its own location of Ippudo, the popular Japanese ramen chain that’s been open for months in Berkeley. It’s nesteld into Yerba Buena Lane, serving hot bowls of ramen, buns, and karaage. There might be a line, so plan accordingly at this fast-paced slurp shop.

18 Yerba Buena Ln

8 Villon

In the gritty heart of Mid-Market, Villon is an oasis with thoughtful dishes from chef Jason Franey in stylish surroundings. Everything-spiced Hawaiian rolls slathered with chicken liver mousse, or okonomiyaki with fermented red cabbage, prawns, and chorizo are equally craveable, and they’re served alongside creative cocktails from BVHospitality. (Make sure to head to the rooftop for an after-dinner drink at newly-opened Charmaine’s.)

San Francisco Proper Hotel

9 Nyum Bai

Owner Nite Yun has brought her Cambodian heritage to Oakland’s Fruitvale neighborhood with bowls of rice noodle soup and 60s-era Cambodian rock music on the speakers, and a colorful interior. Yun has expanded the menu from her first location at Emeryville’s Public Market with options like ginger fried chicken, and even more flavorful stir-fried noodle dishes.

3340 E 12th St Ste. 11
Oakland, CA 94601

10 Kaya Restaurant

Chef Nigel Jones (Kingston 11) has made his first foray across the Bay to SF with a big debut in the former location of Alta in mid-Market. His Caribbean food now has the starring role with dishes like salt fish fritters and oxtail stew, plus rum-based drinks with a nod to tiki. Jones partnered with chef Daniel Patterson on this one to bring his popular cuisine to a neighborhood distinctly lacking in Caribbean flavors.

1420 Market St

11 Barvale

The newest restaurant — pronounced “bar vah-lay” —from Adriano Paganini’s restaurant group Back of the House (Beretta, A Mano, Super Duper) has debuted with Spanish flair on Divisadero. Modeled after the lively tapas bars of Spain, dine on classic tapas, single servings of paella (no 45 minute wait for a huge pan of rice here), and a curated list of gin tonics. It’s got late night dining too, a major plus in sleepy SF.

661 Divisadero St
San Francisco, CA 94117

12 True Laurel

From Lazy Bear chef David Barzelay and bar director Nicolas Torres, this Mission cocktail bar is all about pairing food with drinks, not the other way around. Experience these couplings in a separate tasting menu bar, where a series of cocktails accompanies curated bites (which will debut later in January). In the meantime, order a la carte dishes like a loaded baked potato with miso butter, bacon, and bonito, or a patty melt with dry aged beef, and special sauce to pair with a Stalk Market, made with dry gin, Manzanilla, blanc vermouth, rhubarb pickling liquid, celery, and chamomile. Late night dining is available here, too — the real perfect pairing for drinking.

753 Alabama St
San Francisco, CA 94110
(415) 341-0020

13 Son’s Addition

This 24th Street newcomer is a laid-back neighborhood spot with high-profile food from chef Nick Cobarruvias (previously chef de cuisine of Marlowe) and co-owner Anna Sager Cobarruvias (the two are married). A mixture of well-priced small and large plates like bacon-kimchi deviled eggs or poached chicken served with coconut rice, fermented soybean salsa, and crispy skin are on the seasonally changing menu. And it has an actually affordable wine list, with many bottles priced around $40 or under.

2990 24th St
San Francisco, CA 94110
